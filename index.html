02 The Equity You've Built

Three Layers Already In Place

All three are earned, all three are working. Any new format would compound what's already here.

Operator equity. ChangeMakers exists because you took six legacy firms (Argyle, Believeco, Castlemain and the other Believeco:Partners brands) and folded them into a single 400-person operation in two years. That's a corporate-history piece on its own. The firm now serves Johnson & Johnson, Meta, RBC, Deloitte, Dyson, the Canadian Olympic Committee, Texas Children's Hospital, Yale, Notre Dame, and more, across reputation management, social impact, marketing, organizational change, and agribusiness. The credentials of the firm and the leadership behind it are not in question.

Voice equity. The firm publishes a real point of view, not just service-page copy. The ChangeMakers Reputation Index is a proprietary research product with a verifiable methodology and a thesis worth defending. The Insights blog ships pieces with real titles ("Stop Surviving Change. Start Operating Within It") and a consistent strategic posture. Your own LinkedIn About reads in the first person, with the kind of voice that survives a transcript. The firm has earned the right to speak.

Cultural equity. ChangeMakers serves the kind of clients whose reputation events become the case studies the next generation of comms leaders read about. Black Women's Institute for Health press support. BC First Nations engagement. Olympic-team brand work. These are projects that earn cultural credit, not just impressions, and they put ChangeMakers in the room where the actual reputation decisions get made.

The single area where the public reel is thin is in returning video long-form: founder-led teaching, customer hero films, documentary treatment of the firm's own research. This observation is purely about format range. Those formats can compound the equity you've already built. They also happen to be what the Legendeering framework is built around.

03 Lead With Value

The Legendeering Framework, In One Page

The methodology behind every Talex production. Five directives, one core principle.

Legendeering is Talex's proprietary strategy. The core principle is straightforward: lead with value, always. The brand earns trust by giving without expectation, and that trust grows over time because people keep choosing to watch.

Five directives drive it:

  • ·Give Without Expectation. Value delivered without strings. The kind of generosity that builds loyalty money cannot buy.
  • ·Be Relentlessly Authentic. Say true things. Show real work. Speak like a human being.
  • ·It's Not About You. It's About Them. The audience has to come first. It's all about their wants and needs and making something they will choose to watch.
  • ·Start With Story, Follow With Facts. Story gives information shape, context, and emotional weight. Facts alone rarely move people.
  • ·Consistency Is Key. Trust is built in moments, repeated over time.

ChangeMakers are experts at moving reputation through strategic communications. Talex would just add another layer: documentary-grade long-form video built around the strategies you set. The three ideas below are what that layer looks like for a brand at your scale.

04 Worlds Worth Extending

Three Long-Form Concepts For The ChangeMakers Brand

Each one builds on equity ChangeMakers has already earned. Each one is something only ChangeMakers could make.

01
"The Reputation Index, On Film"
The 2025 Reputation Index analyzed twenty-five companies across five categories of change events. Each category is its own returning documentary episode. One on mergers and acquisitions, with archival footage of the deal cycle and the named CEOs who lived through it. One on leadership transitions. One on brand changes. One on financial changes. One on public crises. You or a rotating ChangeMakers Partner introduces each case in the same role the Index already gives you: the explainer who knows what to look for. The Index has the research. The films give it the form the subject demands.
02
"Six That Became One"
A limited documentary series on the merger that built ChangeMakers. Each of the six legacy firms (Argyle, Believeco, Castlemain and the rest of Believeco:Partners) gets one episode. The founders, the cultures, the work each one brought into the new brand, and the integration calls that produced the firm Mario runs now. You're the connecting thread across the series. The corporate-history story behind the October 2024 rebrand is exactly the kind of thing comms firms help other companies tell. Almost nobody tells it for themselves.
03
"What ChangeMakers Knows"
A teaching-format short series where named ChangeMakers Partners take a single concept they've spent careers on and put it on camera. Mario on growth alchemy and reimagining businesses. Chris Hirsch on creative innovation. Nellie Kim on brand. Megan Gabriel on reputation risk. Robert Gemmill on commercial strategy. The same expertise that already fills the Insights publication, now in the format clients are actually likely to watch. Each Partner gets a portrait and one core teaching. The series doubles as proof of the firm's depth in one place anyone can scroll.
